What I do (for everyone)

I study how the laws of physics and the rules of logic fit together. Imagine a robot that learns from experience. If its “brain” contains both ordinary numbers (like distances) and quantum bits, you run into a problem: the smooth world of geometry and the strange world of quantum mechanics don’t get along. My work creates new mathematical environments where these two worlds can live side by side. This could one day help us build artificial intelligence that is both powerful and completely transparent—you can see exactly why it made a decision.

What I do (for mathematicians)

I construct concrete models of cohesive ∞‑topoi equipped with quantum modalities—idempotent, product‑preserving, strong monoidal comonads—as proposed by Schreiber’s cohesive homotopy type theory. My central example uses finite‑dimensional C*-algebras and the inclusion of commutative subalgebras, yielding a degenerate centre comonad that annihilates non‑commutative structure. I then diagnose this degeneracy and prove a general precomposition obstruction: any coreflective quantum modality on a presheaf topos whose classical core is cartesian forces the Day convolution to become cartesian, collapsing linear logic to classical logic. Current work focuses on resolving this obstruction via mixed distributive laws on orthogonal product sites.
