Cohesive ∞‑Topoi and Quantum Modalities

In the 2010s, Urs Schreiber proposed a synthetic language for quantum mechanics inside a cohesive ∞‑topos—a mathematical universe where discrete and continuous structures coexist naturally. The key ingredient is a quantum modality: an idempotent, product‑preserving, strong monoidal comonad that extracts classical information from quantum systems.

I constructed the first concrete model of this programme using finite‑dimensional C*-algebras and the inclusion of commutative subalgebras. The resulting modality—the centre comonad—faithfully captures the operation of taking the centre of an algebra, but it comes with a price: it annihilates all non‑commutative degrees of freedom. Quantum systems become empty, and the resource‑sensitive linear logic of the quantum topos collapses into ordinary classical logic.

The Precomposition Obstruction

This degeneracy is not an accident. I proved a General Obstruction Theorem: any coreflective quantum modality on a presheaf topos whose classical core is dual to a cartesian category will force the Day convolution to become cartesian, trivialising the Seely isomorphism and collapsing linear logic. The theorem separates the possible models of cohesive quantum logic into two distinct branches—those with trivial decoherence (identity modality) and those that evade the obstruction by using a non‑cartesian algebraic tensor product.

My current work constructs such a non‑degenerate model using an orthogonal product site and a mixed distributive law between the cohesive shape monad and a new quantum comonad. This opens the door to a category of transparent quantum learners—AI systems that combine geometric and quantum data while remaining fully inspectable.
